Japan is famous for a variety of delicious food. Here are some of the most popular Japanese dishes. They are a must-try if you go to Japan!

Sushi

This shouldn't surprise you! Japan is famous for its sushi. Common varieties of sushi include makizushi (sushi rice and fillings rolled up in nori seaweed), nigiri sushi (shaped, bite-size mounds of sushi rice with single slices of raw fish or similar draped over the top) and inarizushi (sushi rice stuffed inside pockets of inari; a type of seasoned, fried tofu).

Udon

Udon is another Japanese staple. Udon can be served in a number of different ways (mixed into stir fries, added to hot pots, served cold with a tsuyu or tentsuyu soup base on the side for dipping), but are most commonly used in noodle soups, where they are served in a savoury soup broth with different garnishes.

Tofu

It would be a crime to not include Tofu in this list! Tofu goes well with practically anything. For those of you who don't know what Tofu is, Tofu is soy milk that has been coagulated, with the resulting curds being pressed into blocks. These blocks come in differing levels of firmness, and can be eaten uncooked (perhaps with a couple of savoury garnishes), boiled in hot pots, or fried into tasty pieces of aburaage and used as a garnish.

Tempura

Miss your fries? Well, I've got something even better. If you enjoy crispy fried foods, then you will love tempura. Tempura are pieces or slices of meat, fish, and/or vegetables that have been covered in a special tempura batter and deep fried until they become crunchy and pale gold in colour.

Yakitori

Yakitori is the Japanese version of kebabs! There are many different types of yakitori, but the most common varieties are momo (chicken thigh), negima (chicken and spring onion), and tsukune (chicken meatballs). Don't worry if you can't remember all those Japanese words. There are usually English menus to help you order!
